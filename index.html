About

I am a 2nd Year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Pittsburgh, advised by Professor Xiang (Lorraine) Li.

Research Interests:

  • Memory Editing in Large Language Models (LLMs): Developing techniques to efficiently update internal knowledge representations for better adaptability and fairness without the computational cost of full finetuning.
  • Commonsense Reasoning: Tackling challenges in grounding language models to improve fairness and reliability in real-world applications.
  • Fairness in AI: Investigating methods to mitigate biases in AI systems, ensuring equitable and reliable model performance across diverse user groups.
  • Conversational AI and NLU: Specializing in Natural Language Understanding for enhancing conversational AI systems, including multi-intent classification, handling out-of-domain queries, and developing real-world applications like Samsung Bixby.

A major milestone in my career was leading the overhaul of the deep learning architecture for Samsung SmartThings. During this time, I worked as a Lead Engineer - NLP at the Samsung Research, where I developed solutions for NLU challenges faced by Samsung’s Conversational AI from research to commercialization.


I earned my Master's in Computer Science and Engineering from IIT Kharagpur, following a Bachelor's in Computer Science and Engineering from NIT Jalandhar.
